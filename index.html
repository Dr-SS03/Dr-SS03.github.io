I am a PhD-trained immunologist with more than 10 years of experience across host–pathogen biology, translational immunology, molecular biology, biomarker-oriented workflows, and scientific communication.

My work sits at the intersection of mechanism and meaning: I design and interpret experiments, synthesize literature, analyze datasets, and turn complex biological findings into manuscripts, reports, figures, presentations, and decision-ready scientific narratives.

That combination makes my profile relevant to academic research groups, biotech and pharma teams, medical writing functions, and consulting-style scientific roles that require deep subject-matter understanding, structured analysis, and clear communication.

02Scientific Profile

The shape of how I think and work

Domain

Immunology & disease biology

My research has focused on how immune systems respond to microbial, inflammatory, and tissue-level perturbations across host–pathogen biology, inflammatory bowel disease, epithelial injury and repair, and immune-mediated disease models.

I am especially interested in questions where disease biology must be resolved across multiple layers at once — cellular phenotypes, tissue context, molecular signaling, and translational relevance.

Bench

Experimental systems

I work with a broad experimental toolkit that includes flow cytometry, ELISA, qPCR, CRISPR/Cas9 perturbation, molecular cloning, organoid systems, mouse models, biomarker readouts, microscopy-adjacent workflows, and structured technical documentation.

Across these systems, I focus not only on execution but also on interpretation, troubleshooting, reproducibility, and the translation of experimental evidence into usable scientific conclusions.

Communication

Scientific writing & interpretation

A major part of my work is scientific communication: drafting manuscripts, abstracts, slide decks, reports, figure legends, technical summaries, progress updates, and grant-related materials for multidisciplinary audiences.

I bring publication support, literature review, data storytelling, and narrative building into the same workflow as the science itself, which allows me to move comfortably between bench research, translational analysis, and medical or scientific writing contexts.
